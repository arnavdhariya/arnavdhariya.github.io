About

I'm an undergraduate at the University of California, Irvine, graduating in June 2027 with a B.S. in Computer Science. Dean's Honor List, 6 quarters.

My research sits at the intersection of database systems, distributed infrastructure, and agentic AI. I currently work on privacy-preserving deletion and inference-aware data management in Sharad's Lab, distributed vector search infrastructure for large-scale agentic workloads in the NetSAIL Lab, energy systems and building automation in CalPlug, and previously hyperspectral imaging and haplotype assembly with Prof. Hayes and Prof. Healy.

I'm always happy to talk about query optimization, storage engines, data deletion, agentic systems, or PhD programs. See contact below.

Papers

Inference-Aware & Privacy-Preserving Deletion in Databases

SIGMOD Workshop · ACM SeQuereDB 2026
Chakraborty, V., Kaminsky, Y., Dhariya, A.A., Mehrotra, S., Naumann, F., Pandey, S.

Addresses the challenge of satisfying Right-to-Be-Forgotten requests in a way that preserves differential privacy guarantees while preventing inference leakage. Introduces ILP-based deletion mechanisms evaluated across diverse UCI ML datasets, with a reproducible benchmarking framework for leakage analysis.

PDF (arXiv)

A Systematic Literature Review of Climate Econometrics Research

SSRN · April 2025
Freedman, H., Foletta, M., Fernandez, G., Sokolova, V., Dhariya, A.A., van der Hoek, A., Tomlinson, B.

A systematic review of 300+ climate econometrics papers, evaluating methodological rigor, reproducibility, and the availability of data and code. Synthesizes trends and gaps in the field to inform future reproducible climate research.
